Welcome to the FAF Beta Balance patchnotes

The changes listed here are all subject to change.

Disclamer: Changes here may be broken or have unforeseen side effects if you encounter anything please report it to the balance team on the Game Bug Reporting Channel on the FAF Discord

All the changes that are currently listed as part of fafbeta allow us to evaluate the new balance in actual games.

We will keep this page as up-to-date as possible and once we feel the changes are ready for the main game in beta we will push the next balance patch.

Economy Structures

In the early game, the primary focus is on raiding expanding engineers. One engineer's death may have potentially devastating consequences. On the other hand, after the expansion phase is over it can be very difficult to find value with light raids. We'd like to encourage more interaction between players by increasing the damage potential of small raids after the expansion phase.

Reducing Mex HP across the board should encourage raiding across most of the game. In particular, the T1 Mex HP reduction allows for T1 bombers to destroy them in just two passes, which should help them find utility outside of their regular engineer-slaying duties.

Torpedo Interaction Overhall

Torpedo bombers

All projectiles dropped by Torpedo Bombers now interact with anti-torpedo defences. This includes both the projectile-based and the flare-based anti-torpedo defences. All torpedo bombers drop their ordinance in a similar arc as strategic bombers.

One exception is that when aiming, a torpedo bomber aims at the unit's current position instead of aiming where the unit is predicted to be when the projectile impacts. This is done to guarantee a valid firing solution so that all torpedo bombers will always drop their ordinance.

Ordinance dropped by torpedo bombers have their velocity reset as they hit the water's surface. This prevents the projectile from immediately impacting the ocean floor when the water is too shallow. The projectile accelerates to full speed in about 3 to 4 seconds.

Torpedo behavior

All projectiles that are considered a torpedo have their properties standardized, with minor factional differences. They used to be all over the place. Roughly speaking:

  • For all factions: each projectile has an acceleration range of 2, with a base acceleration of 5. As a result the acceleration of a projectile is any value between <3, 7>, This prevents the uncanny effect where multiple torpedoes follow the same trajectory. All projectiles have a minimum lifetime of 10 seconds, an ordinance from torpedo bombers has a minimum lifetime of 15 seconds
  • UEF: each torpedo has a turn rate of 165 and a minor 'zig zag' factor.
  • Cybran: each torpedo has a turn rate of 180 and a minor 'zig zag' factor.
  • Aeon: each torpedo has a turn rate of 190 and a decent 'zig zag' factor.
  • Seraphim: each torpedo has a turn rate of 200 and no 'zig zag' factor.

Flare-based torpedo defenses

All flare-based torpedo defences now have a maximum number of torpedoes that they can redirect.

Projectile-based torpedo defenses

All projectile-based torpedo defences act in a similar, smart fashion to tactical missile defences where they try to not overkill a single torpedo.

Global Vision Change

If vision ranges are too short, then players lack the necessary information to make decisions in the game, thus reducing opportunities for skill expression. On the other hand, if vision ranges are too long, then even inattentive players will be able to react to threats easily long ahead of time, thus again reducing opportunities for skill expression. We feel that currently, the game is closer to the former category in the absence of radar, and more in the latter with radar. Buffing vision ranges, while leaving radars intact has several advantages:

  • Allow players to react to threats within a small time window such as when raiding the enemy base.
  • Avoiding circumstances in which units are unable to see what is attacking them.
  • Allowing identification of important radar blips such as ACUs, exps or anti-air, before running head-first into them.

Changes:

  • Vision radius: +15%
  • This Exludes Scouts, Carriers, Atlantis, Tech 1 Arty
